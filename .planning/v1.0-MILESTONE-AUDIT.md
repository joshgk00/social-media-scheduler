## Recommended Path to Milestone Completion

Closing v1.0 requires deciding what scope actually belongs in v1.0.

### Option A — Re-scope v1.0 to delivered work

Mark v1.0 as the foundation milestone (infra, auth, publish worker, queue engine, media, notifications) and move Phases 3, 7, 8, 10, 11 to subsequent milestones (v1.1, v2.0, etc.). Strong fit because:
- Phases 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 9 form a coherent, runnable system: a user can authenticate, schedule posts (via API), publish through a worker, manage queues, attach media, and receive notifications.
- The unstarted phases are user-facing UX layers (Phase 3 post composition, Phase 7/8 multi-platform, Phase 10 bulk, Phase 11 polish) that are sensibly grouped into a separate milestone.
- All closed-loop integration gaps are confirmed closed.

### Option B — Execute the missing phases

Plan and execute Phases 3, 7, 8, 10, 11. This is a substantial body of work (70 requirements) and likely requires re-discussing and re-planning each phase.

### Recommended

**Re-scope v1.0** (Option A). The work that is done is high-quality and forms a complete system within its own boundaries. Pushing the unstarted phases into a v1.1 milestone preserves accurate milestone semantics and lets v1.0 close cleanly.
